📜 Scroll I

In an age before ash claimed the skies, there was a warrior who would not yield.
He walked through kingdoms as though fate itself bent to his will.
Where others saw endings, he saw only another step forward.
Even death, it was said, would hesitate before him.

📜 Scroll II

Far from the light of men, a mage wove power from silence and bone.
He spoke not to kings, but to things that listened from beneath the world.
Knowledge came to him at a price he no longer remembered paying.
In time, even his own name lost meaning.

📜 Scroll III

Their paths were never meant to cross, yet fate has a cruel sense of symmetry.
The warrior hunted the unnatural, and the mage had become precisely that.
Between them stood no army, no witness, no mercy.
Only inevitability.

📜 Scroll IV

Steel met spell beneath a sky that would not forget.
The earth split as if it too feared what it held above.
Neither struck to wound, but to erase.
And neither stepped back.

📜 Scroll V

When the silence came, it did not belong to victory.
Two bodies lay broken where power had burned itself empty.
No breath, no voice, no triumph remained.
Only something… unfinished.

📜 Scroll VI

But death is not an ending when will refuses to release it.
From the ruin, something rose that was not what it had been.
The flesh remembered strength, but not humanity.
And the soul… was no longer alone.

📜 Scroll VII

A voice lingered where no mouth remained.
It did not scream, nor plead, nor fade.
It waited, patient as rot beneath stone.
And when it spoke, it did not ask.

📜 Scroll VIII

The creature that walked wore the shape of the fallen warrior.
Yet something within it leaned toward violence as if it were home.
Each life taken fed a hunger it could not name.
And something unseen… fed with it.

📜 Scroll IX

The voice grew clearer with every ending.
It spoke of purpose, of direction, of becoming more.
It guided the hand that once chose freely.
And slowly, choice itself became quieter.

📜 Scroll X

There are whispers that do not belong to the living.
There are wills that survive without form.
The mage had not perished as the world believed.
He had simply… changed his shape.

📜 Scroll XI

Bound not by flesh, but by proximity to power, he endured.
Each act of violence tethered him closer, made him stronger.
He no longer needed a body—only a vessel.
And the warrior had become… perfect.

📜 Scroll XII

Now they walk as one, though only one still pretends to resist.
One seeks redemption, the other only continuation.
But with every step, every kill, every surrender…
It becomes harder to tell which one remains.
